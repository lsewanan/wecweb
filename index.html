Mission

A physician is obligated to consider more than a diseased organ, more than even the whole man. He must view the man in his world. — Harvey Cushing

The Wednesday Evening Clinic (WEC) is a student-run longitudinal primary care clinic in Yale's Primary Care Center that has been in operation for over 50 years. It provides primary care services to diverse members of our New Haven community.

Integrated Primary Care

  • WEC functions as a group practice, and is divided into three patient care teams, each supervised by a designated team attending.

  • Students manage general health maintenance including: basic OB/GYN care, age-appropriate screening, urgent visits for acute problems, chronic conditions (such as diabetes, obesity, and hypertension), and coordinate care for complicated problems seen by specialists.

    • Service Learning

  • Most patients in our clinic are from low-income households with many barriers to healthcare access.

  • You may be your patient’s only access to medical care, and you have the potential to improve the lives of your patients and of their families.

    • Longitudinal Clinical Experience

  • All patient care is provided by the medical student assigned to that patient, or by one of their student colleagues.

  • You will be listed as the PCP for a panel of 30-40 patients.

    • Continuing Medical Education

  • Students will develop clinical skills while actively caring for patients under the close guidance and mentorship of PCC attendings.

  • here will be opportunities to prepare case reports for publication, and partake in primary care-related outcomes and quality improvement research.

    • Benefits of being a WEC Provider

    1. You will be listed as the PCP for a panel of 30-40 patients.

    2. You will work under the close guidance and mentorship of PCC attendings, developing your clinical skills while actively caring for your cohort of patients.

    3. You will have excellent opportunities for preparing case reports for publication, and partaking in primary care-related outcomes and quality improvement research. Mentorship with faculty attendings is readily available and project ideas are always welcomed.

    4. Completion of a one-year rotation at WEC grants primary care elective credit , a necessity for students interested in applying for California residencies.

    Requirements to be a WEC provider

    • 1. We ask that all students commit to being a PCP for at least one year; this ensures that patients and providers benefit from the longitudinal aspects of primary care. During this one-year commitment, students are able to take up to 3 months of time away from clinic in order to complete PhD requirements, sub-internships, and for vacation time.
    • 2. We strongly recommend that students complete the Primary Care and Mental Health combined clerkship prior to joining WEC. Additional rotations in internal medicine, OB/GYN are also recommended.
